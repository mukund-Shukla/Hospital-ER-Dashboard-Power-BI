# Hospital-ER-Dashboard-Power-BI
This project analyzes Emergency Room (ER) patient data to identify patterns in patient visits, wait times, satisfaction levels, referral trends, and demographics. The dataset covers April 2023 – October 2024 and includes 9,000 unique patient records.

🛠 Tools & Technologies
Power BI – Dashboard Development & Visualization
Power Query – Data Cleaning & Transformation
DAX – Calculated measures and KPIs
Healthcare Dataset – Emergency Room Patient Records

📈 Key Insights

⏱ Patient Wait Time & Satisfaction
The average patient wait time was 35.3 minutes, suggesting potential opportunities to improve operational efficiency.
The average patient satisfaction score was 4.99 / 10, indicating moderate satisfaction levels and highlighting the need for improved patient experience.

🏥 Departmental Referrals
5,400 patients did not require referrals after ER consultation.
Among referred patients, the most common departments were:
General Practice – 1,840 cases
Orthopedics – 995 cases
Physiotherapy – 276 cases
Cardiology – 248 cases
These trends suggest that general medical consultations and musculoskeletal issues are among the most frequent ER follow-ups.

📅 Peak Patient Traffic
The busiest days were:
Monday – 1,377 patients
Saturday – 1,322 patients
Tuesday – 1,318 patients
The peak visiting hours were:
11 AM
1 PM
7 PM
11 PM
These patterns highlight the importance of adequate staffing during peak hours.

👥 Patient Demographics
The largest patient age group was Adults (30–39 years) with 1,200 patients.
Followed by Young Adults (20–29 years) with 1,188 patients.
The data indicates that working-age adults make up the majority of ER visits.

🌍 Race Distribution
White – 2,571 patients
African American – 1,951 patients
Multiracial – 1,557 patients
Asian – 1,060 patients
1,030 patients declined to identify their race
This demonstrates a diverse patient population served by the hospital.

🏥 Admission Patterns
4,612 patients were admitted.
4,604 patients were treated and released.
This indicates an almost equal split between admitted and non-admitted patients.

📌 Conclusion

The analysis reveals several important operational insights:
Emergency departments experience high patient volumes during specific days and hours.
Patient wait times and satisfaction scores indicate room for improvement.
General practice and orthopedics are the most frequent referral departments.
ER visits are dominated by working-age adults, suggesting lifestyle or work-related health concerns.
These insights can help hospital administrators optimize staffing, improve patient care workflows, and enhance overall healthcare delivery.
